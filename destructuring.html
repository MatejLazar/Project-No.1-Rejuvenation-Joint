Welcome to the
Destructuring Dojo!

Here you will be introduced to 15 different postures which after carried out correctly will bring the deep relaxation to the whole muscular system.

You will be asked to spend atleast 20 seconds in each posture, breathing in for 5, holding for 5, breathing out for 5 and then holding for another 5 seconds.!

It is completely fine to spend more than 5 seconds on each section of the particular posture, reducing your time down from 5 seconds will definitelly decrease the potential benefits. It is highly recomended to exhale on the most difficult parts of the stretch, and with the breath go deeper into the stretch, that way the pain will be more berable and you will be able to stretch further.

Destructuring sequence can server better than a strong cup of coffe. Morning session will energize you and get you ready for the day, plus you will have a little victory under your belt. One must have a quite strong power of will to do it first thing in the morning, as Mark Twain famously said: "If the first thing you do in the morning is eat a live frong, you can go through the rest of the day knowing the worst is behind you." Your frog is your worst task, or maybe even the task you will most certainly procrastinate and eventually skip as the day progresses.

Visulisation plays an enormous role in each of the postures, and is of a great benefit in each field of life too. Practicing visualiation and by training your imagination here in the Destructuring Dojo, you will develop the great skill which you will be able to use while performing other tasks, and by doing so, your performance is going to increase significantly.

Postures

1. Hill Drill

Get into the squat position and stretch one leg to the side, put the weight on the heel while holding the balance on the other foot.

The balancing foot should be flat on the ground, but if not applicable, you can maintain the balance on the ball of your foot, and then each time you exhale, lower that foot closer to the ground in order to increase the flexibility.

To advance in this exercise, you need to make sure to keep stretching the leg forward and allow your bottom to go further down. On the exhale, reach with the closer hand towards the arch of your foot.



2. Curlicue

From "Hill Drill move onto the stretched leg, bend the knee and push other leg far back, balancingit on your toes."

To achieve full potential of this exercise, you must imagine the front knee being pushed forward and the heel of the back leg pulled backwards.

An advanced practicioners can place the rear knee on the floor and imagine the pelvic gridle meeting the floor. Once you reach the deepest stretch, place the closer hand onto your knee, and twist the back while slightly pushing the hand into the knee.



3. Latch

From "Curlicue" you lock your body with "Latch" by twisting the rear foot on the side. You no longer keep the keep the body weight on the front knee, instead, you move your upper body to the center of the posture and allow the pelvis to slowly drop down toward the floor.

In order to perform this, or any other posture perfectly, the use of the imagination is a sheer necessity. In this case you need to give yourself four mental directionings by imagioning knee being pushed forward and up, rear foot being pulled in the opposite direction and having an extra weight being placed on the top of your pelvis while simoultaneously having a head and neck being pulled towards the ceeling by an invisible string.

It is perfectly fine to give yourself first two directions, and as you grow your ability to maintain the multi focus, you can then add third and forth direction.



4. Mantis Chill

To get into "Mantis Chill" posture, you begin by standing straight in a wide stance, direct your feet sideways and squat as low as your groin allows you. Keep your upper body straight in the alignment with the pelvis. Once you reach the limit, the imagination and conscious directioning starts playing their part.

To get that little extra out of this posture, you imagine the each knee flowing in it's direction towards the side walls and your pelvic floor sinking to the ground.



5. Lotus

In "Lotus" you just sit down and connect the feet together. The key to perform this exercise correctly is to pull the heels all the way to your crotch. Depending on the level of your flexibility push the knees down to your limit. When you reach the point of the deepest stretch, allow your body to move forward gaining even deeper strech and then hold.

This posture doens't require the imagination to be used, you can simply follow the instructions to reach the point of maxiumum streatch and the as you ehale go that extra mile, face the pain and hold.



6. Dying Cockroach

"Dying Cockroach" position begins by lying down on your back. You lift your legs up shaping your body into a letter "L", next you slightly bend your knees and grab the arch of your beet. Now here is the trick; you got to pull down the feet while simoultaneously pushing your hands away with the force of the legs.

Mostly all of the postures when performed correctly and in depth should produce tremour. Like in the previous posture, there is not much imagination you can do except imagine your bottom being pulled away from the direction you push your hands towards.



7. Californian Wave

Just like a "Dying Cockroach" this posture begins by laying on your back. First you thrust both legs over your head while maintaining the balance on very top of your back. The neck and head also play a role of a balancing variable in this posture. Once you have reached the crux of "Californian Wave", you have to focus on straightening your legs by pushing the heels toward the back wall while imagioning your bottom moving in the opposite direction.

It is much easier to get a tremour in this posture than in any other, so we recommend you to practice it as a stand-alone exercise just to get the feeling of proper tremour. It is enough to stay in tremour for 3 seconds to gain the benefits of deep muscle relaxation.



8. The Clock

"The Clock" is the third posture that begins by laying on your back. What differentiates this posture from previous two is that you don't lift the legs as before, but instead you bring your knees as close to your chest as possible, and from this point twist the hips on the side while keeping your upper back straight on the ground. The next step is to stretch the top leg.

When you reach the maximum stretch use the visulisation technique and think you heel and bottom into the opposite directions. Repeat the same steps for the other side.



9. Rocky Horse

Unlike in the previous postures, this one begins by laying flat on the stomach. First you must bend you knees and take a grip of the bridge of you feet, then lift the chest off the floor while simoultaneously pushing your hands away with the force of the legs. To reach the maximum potential of the stretch this posture provides you have to imagine your chest moving toward the front wall while your shoulders are moving away in the opposite direction.



10. Healthy Cow

"Healthy Cow's" starting position is on all four. In the first part of the this posture you stretch your back and spine by slowly arching your lower and upper back like a cat. On the the exale, drop your chest abruptly. The next step is to allow the chest to sink further down. Imagine it as though the chest is seperating from the rest of the body. Your head and neck should be relaxed and hanging. At this point you should feel a very deep stretch around shoulderblades. The key is to move deeper into the pain.



11. Twister

"Twister" basically comes out of the "Healthy Cow". To get that unique deep stretch "Twister" does, you begin by slowly bending your elbows and moving chest toward the floor. Once you're half way from the floor, reach with one arm underneath the oppsite pitarm and keep stretching it further. Imagine the arm moving futher away even though you think you have reached your limit, allow your torso to twist as you're moving the arm away from you. This stretch is so good for your spine. You should feel the deep, deep strech in your back.



12. Blourney Stone

"Blourney Stone" is a great posture in so many ways. It is easu to perform and you get to the deep stretch quickly and it hits different areas of your body. The main stretch occurs in the hips, chest, shoulders, front of the neck and underchin muscle. All you have to do is to kneel down and reach for Achilies tendon. The next step is to move hips and chest toward the front wall and allow head to hang back.

To reach the deeper stretch use the technique of visalisation as in the previous postures. Imagine the chest moving up and away from your body.



13. Blue Bloded Baboon

"Blue Blooded Baboon" can be dismantled into three section. Groundwork, hang, and rise. In ground work one has to stand straight and bend enough to place palms flat on the floor. You are allowed to bend your need little bit too.

Once you have got into the monkey pose, push your bottom up and away at about 90 degree angle while maintaining the hands on the ground. Try to relax the body in the spite of pain.

When you breathe out, remove your hands from the ground, place them on your elbows, straighten the legs and simply hang. The trick here is to release all the tension and move deeper into the stretch.

The final part of this posture is the rise, you slowly rise up vertebra by vertebra.



14. FG Bents

"FG Bents" is perhaps the most difficult exercuse on the list, but once you get a hang of it it won't be as troublesome as it might be on the beginning.

The first step is to stand in a narrow stance, feet together. Stretch you arms toward the ceeling with a finger-gun gesture. The trick here is to imagine lats, shoulders and arms being pulled away in sky while everything else is falling through the ground. When you reach that deep stretch, send your hips to one side and lean sideways in an opposite direction. You have to make sure you don't twist the torso as you bend, Keep it nice and parralel with the front wall. You also got to keep sending the arms and lats away through the whole process.

Once you reach the point of the maximum stretch, stay there for a few seconds, focus on your breath while still having the focus on the directions you're giving to yourself. Slowly return to the starting position and do the same for the other side.

The second step is to bend backwards. A very important tip here is to avoid putting the pressure on the lower-back. You use very little of lower-back movement just in order to find the focal point in the middle of your torso.

This might sound little bit confusing, but the consitant practice will get you there. From that focal point you band backward while simoultaneously pushing the front of the focal point forward. When you reach your limit, slowly rise up into the starting position.

Now as a final step, you're going to stretch your arms once again the same way as before but this time, lean forward. When you get to a 90 degree angle, with the power of imagionation stretch the arms further and push the pelvis backwards. On the exale, drop the arms, bend one knee and bounce against the pressure. Swap the knees and do repeat.

Finally squat down into a deep squat with your feet flat on the ground, allow your back to go forward into the strech. Imagine your head and spine moving into the ground.



15. NexFlex

"Nexflex" is j a boundle of neck stretches which will definitelly turn your life around if you have a stiff neck or any beck pain.

First get into the standard lotus position, cross legged. Then stretch both arms sideways and touch the floor with the tips of your fingers. The next step is to turn your head to the side until you reach the limit. Ideally you chin will be in line with the shoulder, now slightly tilt your head toward the floor and keep pushing with very little force against the pressure. slowly return your head to a starting position and do the same for the other side.

In the second part of "NeckFlex" you will tilt your head in a way that you come as close to your shoulder with the ear as possible, hold it there and on the exale place your hand over the head and allow the gravity to do it's work. Do the same for the other side and finally, let your head hang forward, place both hand over the back of your head and let the gravity pull it down. In each of these stretches it is very usefull to focus on the pain and allow yourself to "go" with it. To imagine you're diving deeper into it.
